Important Dates

We are pleased to announce the important dates for PDCAT 2025. Please mark your calendars!
All deadlines are based on Anywhere on Earth (AoE), at midnight on the specified date.

  • Paper Submission Deadline: 1 August 2025 15 August 2025 (extended)
  • Notification of Acceptance: 15 September 2025
  • Camera-Ready Submission Deadline: 30 September 2025
  • Conference Dates: 22 – 24 November 2025 (AEST)

Paper Submissions

Authors are invited to submit original and unpublished research papers in English. Submissions should present novel research results or significant practical experiences. Submissions will be selected based on their originality, timeliness, significance, relevance, and clarity of presentation. The papers should be formatted according to the Springer LNCS format. The submission must be in PDF format.

The manuscript should not exceed 14 pages in the Springer LNCS style, including references. An optional appendix may be included, but it will not be counted toward the page limit.

Springer’s LNCS LaTeX templates are available in OverLeaf (Latex Template).

For detailed author guidelines, please refer to Springer's Information for Authors of Computer Science Proceedings.

We invite all prospective authors to submit your manuscripts through the PDCAT2025 submission portal, hosted on Submit

Conference Proceedings & SCI Journals

All accepted and presented papers will be published in the Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series by Springer. Authors of accepted papers are required to complete the Springer copyright form and ensure that their final manuscripts comply with Springer's formatting guidelines.

Selected papers presented at the conference will be invited for extended publication in special issues of SCI-indexed journals. Here is a tentative list of journals:

  • Tsinghua Science and Technology (Impact Factor: 5.2)
  • Human-centric Computing and Information Sciences (Impact Factor: 3.9)
  • Computers and Electrical Engineering (Impact Factor: 4.0)
  • CCF Transactions on High Performance Computing (Impact Factor: 1.3)

These invitations will be extended based on the quality, originality, and relevance of the papers to the respective journal scopes.
